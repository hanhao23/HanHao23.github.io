About Me

I am a post doctoral researcher at the Caliber Lab in the Department of Psychology, New Mexico State University. I received my Ph.D. in Applied Cognitive Psychology at Claremont Graduate University in 2022. My primary research interests include the impact of working memory on selective attention, individual differences in cognitive ability, and statistical methods (e.g., structural equation modeling, item response theory, and psychometric network analysis) for psychometric and cognitive modeling of human complex cognition. I am also interested in data analytic and visualization with R and Python.
