An Examination of Domain-Specificity Differences in Complex Span Tasks through Item Response Theory

Ester Navarro, Kevin Rosales, Han Hao, Andrew R. A. Conway
June 2020
Item Tracelines from a Generalized Partial Credit Model of Symmetry Span Task

Abstract

Working memory (WM) is the ability to maintain a goal in mind and manipulating information in the face of concurrent action (Baddeley & Hitch, 1974). While complex span tasks have been used widely to measure WM, there is still debate regarding the extent to which different complex span tasks vary based on their domain-specificity (Dumontheil et al., 2019). Item response theory (IRT) is a largely unexplored approach that could help further explore such potential variation. Two spatial and two verbal complex span tasks were examined using IRT. The findings show that, comparing to visuo-spatial tasks, verbal tasks have (1) less variety in item difficulties and (2) have worse item discriminations. The results also show that later blocks within the same length tend to be easier, especially for long items. The results suggest that the domain-specificity of complex span tasks might have a heavier weight than previously thought on WM scores.
